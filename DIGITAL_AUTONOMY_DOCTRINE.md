# Digital Autonomy Doctrine

I2P Connect exists to make digital autonomy feel normal, hopeful, useful, calm, and community-driven.

Privacy is dignity. The product should help people understand and control their communication without fear-based messaging, conspiracy framing, or unsupported promises.

## Principles

- Local control before cloud convenience.
- Measured status before confident labels.
- Responsible use before risky actions.
- Beginner clarity before expert jargon.
- Open-source trust before growth shortcuts.
- Honest limitations before aspirational claims.
- Community resilience before spectacle.

## Product Tone

Use language that is calm, plain, and practical. I2P Connect should feel like a trustworthy local tool, not a dark pattern, fear funnel, or elite-only artifact.

Prefer:

- privacy as dignity
- hope over fear
- responsible use
- simple onboarding
- least-privilege architecture
- local-first storage
- reviewable implementation
- transparent limitations

Avoid:

- fear-based messaging
- conspiracy framing
- criminal symbolism
- hacker-only aesthetics
- unsupported anonymity claims
- unsupported encryption claims
- unsupported promises of real-time video
- public exposure of I2P control or admin ports

## Honest Limits

Guaranteed anonymity, full E2EE, SASE, zero-trust, and real-time video are not promised in this greenfield foundation.

Future releases may add private messaging, encrypted audio notes, contact verification, and I2P transport integration only when implementation, tests, and documentation support the claims.
